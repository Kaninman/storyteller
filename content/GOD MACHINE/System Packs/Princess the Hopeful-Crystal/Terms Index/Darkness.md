# Darkness Everywhere
The [[All-Consuming Darkness]] is not a “new” and distinct siloed-off phenomenon in the Chronicles of Darkness. It’s as omnipresent as fear itself. Other beings run into it all the time and don’t know it’s anything separate from how the inexplicable world works. Werewolves run into Nightscapes and think “this is a very strange Wound in the hisil,” and try to fix it the best they can without knowing the full scope of how it has to be fixed. Vampires, Changelings, the Unchained, all run into Darkspawn and think “these are more of those Horrors that just follow their own rules and don’t make sense.” 

And it goes the other way, too. Though on a different “frequency,” the Darkness is also expressed through the idiom of other kinds of magic. The Darkness is everywhere—and whatever idiom it comes from, it spreads the taint of the Darkness because it spreads fear. Any supernatural abilities that directly inflict fear, like a vampire’s Nightmare discipline, will ping Sensitivity like Pure Darkness, because it pretty much is Pure Darkness. At the Storyteller’s discretion, and particularly in a crossover Chronicle, other types of supernatural phenomena might trigger a Princess’s Sensitivity as well—they might detect Pandorans as they do Darkspawn, or feel a Mummy’s awakening like an emotional earthquake.

In these cases, the Princess’s Darkness sensitivity is still inferior to whatever means that supernatural creature has of detecting or interacting with its related supernatural phenomenon, it’s harder to detect than Pure Darkness, and it doesn’t inflict Chronic Sensitivity as often. No numbers are given for these because they’ll all be up to Storyteller fiat anyway.
