This is an advanced Upgrade. It counts as two Upgrades when applied to a Charm.

Illusions are about misdirection, so why not take it a step further? Why should you have to decide where the queen is before the mark picks a card? A Tricky Illusion must be used with an image based on an object or willing person, and it must be applied to another object of about the same size The image and the original subject are both at the same time. So long as neither potential image does anything impossible (such as flying, if you don’t have a means of making it fly), you don’t have to decide which is the image and which is the real one until something dispels it. If you have multiple identical images from Versatile Illusion, you don’t have to decide which is the real one until the second to last image is dispelled. If the image is of another person, they will direct both of them and decide which one is real, not you.
