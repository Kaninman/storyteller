A Princess can take the Mentor Merit like any other character to represent a relationship with another person in the real world who offers guidance and help. However, a Princess also has the option of taking her Queen as a Mentor, which has different rules. The dot rating of a Queen Mentor does not represent their power and willingness to help you—those would be seven or eight dots no matter what. And it’s not like they have very limited time, because in dreams things can take as long as they want. Instead, because the Queens are Gale-Blind, the dot rating in Queen mentor represents how close your relationship is and thus how much they are able to remember that is particular to you. They can’t use outside knowledge and most of the Princesses they see aren’t real, so it all comes down to how much they can recall about your particular relationship and history when they meet you. At one dot, each time you meet is like the first time for them. 

At two dots, it’s like the first time meeting but they’ve been told about you through a mutual friend. 
At three dots, you’re someone they know, but haven’t seen in a long time. 
At four and five dots, they’re ready to jump in to whatever problem is facing you right away; at five dots you’re practically roommates. Your Queen Mentor requires no favors from you other than that you continue to carry out your Calling in their name.

Queens cannot give material support in the real world or provide information that you didn’t give them, but they CAN guide you through your own skills to reach your own conclusions. Choose three Skills your relationship with your Queen focuses on. Once per session, you can choose to contact your Queen (or have your Queen contact your character) in the Dreamlands to get counsel. If what you ask for is something the Queen can do in that meeting without access to outside information—help you put together some clues you have, or console you after an awful breakup—then she does so and is considered to roll automatic successes equal to your dots in Queen Mentor. If you need her help with a Skill that you will use, and can plan for it and walk through it ahead of time, your Queen’s guidance will allow you to score automatic successes equal to your Skill dots or your dots in Queen Mentor, whichever is lower.

The Twilight Queens are not Gale-Blind, but two have their own extenuating circumstances limiting their own ability to help. The Queen of Storms can only be taken as a one-dot Mentor, because the only counsel she gives is through visions of her violence. The Queen of Tears can only be taken as a Mentor up to 3 dots, because she only has so much time for each of her disciples. The Queen of Mirrors, however, can be given any number of dots as Mentor, even above 5. You’re the one and only True Heir, after all
