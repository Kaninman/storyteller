A circle of friends is of paramount importance to keep yourself grounded in the real world and from spiraling off into despair. Dots in the Circle Merit represent not just your larger circle of friends, but your ability to connect with and let your guard down around them, whether they are members of your nakama or other people you know.

The Circle Merit allows a Princess to play out a short scene of interaction with her circle of friends, either by playing it out ahead of time or “flashing back” to an appropriate scene during the middle of a serious scene. She can then call on the benefit at any point later in the chapter.

Having played out the Circle scene grants one of two benefits. One, you may refill a number of Wisps equal to your dots in Circle. Two, you may reroll any one failed Compromise, with a bonus equal to your dots in Circle. This can convert an automatic Belief loss from failing while Emotionally Invested into a Compromise roll for an equivalent failure (rolling 7, 5, or 3 dice as appropriate).

There are two criteria for the scene to grant either mechanical effect: one, it must substantially involve interaction with one’s friends, and two, it must have absolutely nothing whatsoever to do with your supernatural mission. You don’t gain the saving roll from talking about how you did everything you could to save that poor victim. You get the saving roll for attempting to play matchmaker with your hopelessly awkward bestie, pulling an all-nighter studying for a test you have no clue about, getting obsessed with winning a radio call-in contest and coming up with schemes to increase your odds, putting together the coolest party in the city, desperately covering up the coolest party in the city when you realize your friend’s parents are coming home early…

You know. Antics.

Multiple player characters can (and should) gain the benefit of the Circle Merit from playing in the same single scene.
