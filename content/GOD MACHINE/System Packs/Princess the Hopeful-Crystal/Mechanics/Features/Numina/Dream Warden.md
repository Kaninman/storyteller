All attempts by the Lucid to detect the presence or significance of this entity are at a -5 penalty, and they have to deliberately make the attempt. Passive observation or detection abilities cannot detect the Warden. Figments automatically and tirelessly carry out the Warden’s orders. This part of the ability costs no Essence to maintain.

This entity can also attempt to bind Princesses in [[Campaign- City of Sin/Locations/The Dreamlands & Dark World/The Dreamlands|The Dreamlands]], sealing away their knowledge of the real world and their real lives and replacing it with a dream. If it attempts to do this through physical contact, it rolls Power + Finesse vs Composure + Inner Light (if it’s possible for it to do this to things with other Supernatural Tolerance stats, nobody’s recognized it). If it attempts to do this through indirect actions by manipulating Figments into messing with the target, then the target rolls Composure + Inner Light with a -1 penalty for each roll they made since they last failed.

Success for the Warden or failure for the target locks one Galemark the target already has. It can’t create new Galemarks, but the [[Gale-Wracker]] Numina can, which Wardens almost always also have. The Galemark becomes a Persistent Condition that does not grant Beats and can’t be healed normally; it represents memories being confused between real life and the Dreamlands, with more important memories being mixed up with higher Galemarks. 

At 5 locked Galemarks, the target has forgotten almost everything of their real life and replaced it with a Lucid existence, and their real-world body slips into a coma. Killing the Warden unlocks and cures all locked Galemarks, and using magic to heal them requires the healer to fight off or defeat the efforts of that specific Warden in some way.
