# Signets
Signets are a special type of magic taught by a Queen and her followers, not usually self-discovered. Signets grant abilities that support and express the philosophy of the Queen that grants them. They’re not as flexible as the ability to create your own Charm out of Upgrades; but they are more focused, can do certain things Charms can’t (or at least can’t do easily), and supplement your capabilities in a different way. Signets can be freely used in your normal form without appearing magical, unless they specify they can only be used while Transformed or only apply to something you can only do while Transformed. By adopting and sympathizing with the worldview of a Queen, some of her vision of how the world should work becomes how the world does work. You can boost mundane attributes, gather more advantages for acting in line with the Signet’s philosophy, modify how you cast your Charms, employ specialized abilities like “recall any library you’ve ever been in” or “surround yourself with helpful animals and familiars,” and cap it all off with awe-inspiring power transformations or devastating ultimate abilities. At each rank of a Signet, there are two options for a power. Pick only one of them. Both of the options generally address the same type of thing; for example, at rank 1, all

Signets give you a choice between pending Wisps to buff certain mundane rolls, or gaining more Wisps and bonuses when you spend Willpower on mundane rolls that meet certain criteria. It’s a choice between being better at spending resources, or better at gaining them. At rank 2, Signets give you the option to choose an Elemental Attunement or a way to cast a Charm to gain special bonuses, a choice of how to change up your spellcasting. If you want to get both of the powers at a given rank of a Signet, you have to take another dot in the Signet and forfeit the power you’d gain at the next level. So, if you want both Rank 2 abilities of a Signet, you have to have three dots and spend the third dot on the second Rank 2 ability in lieu of one of the Rank 3 powers. Since the highest a Signet goes is 5 dots, you’ll never be able to have both Rank 5 abilities at once.

Changing the “loadout” of your Signets is possible, if your interpretation of the Signet’s philosophy changes (or if you, as a player, decide you don’t like your loadout and want to respec.) It can’t be done quickly, but between major chapters when a character has a lot of downtime, they can find themselves discovering their powers developing in a different direction, and reassign their Signet choices at each rank, including the expressions of elemental-attuned and infused.


# Elemental Attuned
Each Signet has an option you can take to designate some of your Charms as elemental-attuned to a named, specific element. Elemental attunement is not an Upgrade, but a modification to the basic Charm. Elemental-attuned Charms can only act through or on things in line with that element. This limits the function of what they can do because of this necessary correspondence; if you want an elemental ability that adds power to the Charm, you want elemental-infused from later in the Signet. Each Signet provides a list of examples of how an elemental-attuned Charm might work. Twilight attunements can work through the signature emotion of their Princesses, but Radiant ones can’t and are locked to the literal expression of that element. You’ll choose 2 charms to be attuned, and can add more by spending 1 XP each.

Choose the specific expression of how your Charm works through an element when you make it attuned; you don’t pick a new expression every time you cast the Charm. If you have a Fuoco-attuned Detect that requires you to stare into a bonfire to see visions, and you can’t get one of those, then you can’t change the elemental attunement to require something else like burning something with a sympathetic connection. Sometimes, the elemental attunement won’t be a problem at all, but sometimes, it could be a huge problem.

The benefit for all elemental-attuned Charms is the same: add your dots in the associated Signet to your dice pool, apply an additional free Upgrade, and you may cast the Charm for 1 less Wisp a number of times per chapter equal to your dots in that Signet but still get the Buster Overcharge as if you spent the Wisp. You can use the Charm without the elemental attunement if you really, really, really need something the element can’t handle, but it’s costly: not only do you not get the attunement benefits, but you need to spend an additional Willpower to cast it (unless you have [[Flexible Attunement]], p. 85).

You can designate the same Charm as attuned to more than one element if you have more than one ability that grants attunement. If you do, pick which elemental attunement applies when you cast the Charm, you don’t have to meet the criteria for both. If you do manage to do something that is in line with both elements (like combining a fire attunement and a water attunement to cast a Charm that uses hot steam), you apply both bonuses, and it only counts against the number of “free wisps” you get from one Signet. Though your elemental attunements are fixed when you pick them, you don’t have to choose ahead
of time how to combine them, so long as you can come up with something that makes sense in the moment.

# Elemental Infused
Each Signet also has an option, further in its development, to infuse elemental power into your Charms. Taking this Signet gives you an Infusion Upgrade, which is an Upgrade that you can now apply to all of your Charms like any other. Each Infusion Upgrade has the same basic text: it infuses the Charm with the power of the element, granting it an additional ability or capability tied to that element.

Just like Elemental Attuned Charms, the list of what possible mechanical effects this can have is too large to fit here (it’d double the length of the Charms section at the very least since you can apply them to Charms in multiple ways), so only a list of example abilities is given. You can and should make your own, aiming for an effect that’s roughly about as powerful as a normal Upgrade. It is an Upgrade, so like with elemental-attuned, you choose how the elemental power is expressed when you take it, you are not able to change it every time you use it. It’s one Upgrade, not ten Upgrades in one.

Also like Elemental Attuned Charms, you’ll pick two Charms to get the infusion, and pay 1 XP for each further one you want to add. The Twilight versions of these infusions can apply powers from the signature emotion of the Princess, but the Radiant ones cannot.

# Signet Types
## Acqua
Acqua is the Signet of the Queen of Currents, controlling water and ice and empowering a clear mind and rational approach to problems. Water from rain grows crops and quenches our thirst, but water in currents—water in rivers and oceans—bears vessels containing new developments and new ideas. Trade and the exchange of ideas is what truly draws the Queen to the element of water, though the crystal clarity of pure and untainted liquid certainly helps. She developed it not only through extensive personal research and study, but also by conferring with everyone she could to hear from as broad and diverse a set of viewpoints as possible. She’s had a major hand in developing EVERY Radiant Signet, at least their modern forms; the Queen of Currents is probably the reason Signet Charms for the Radiant don’t stop at 3 or 4 dots and how they were able to catch up to the Twilight Signets in so little time.

It is right and natural that each of the Radiant Queens are supposed to only teach their Signet to those who uphold their values. The Queen of Currents, however, is… extremely flexible on that front. She’ll teach the first 3 dots to just about anyone, including followers of Tears, Storms, and Mirrors who have no intention of turning their back on their Queen or their values. She thinks that being able to do things with a clear mind will help anyone discover that her path is the best way forward, but she also wants as wide a range of feedback as possible on how the Signet is working and how it could be improved.

## Aria
Aria is the Signet of the Queen of Spades, affecting air and wind and empowering a flexible, expressive, unconventional approach. Air goes everywhere, respecting no borders or boundaries. If there is any way out or through something, air will find it. Air is light. Air is here, then it’s gone. Air is ever present, yet escapes notice. And nobody thinks about how critically they need air until they start to lose it. Freedom and flexibility draw the Queen of Spades to the element of air: to be free as a bird, to blow like the wind, to see a thunderhead on the horizon and crank the engines on the Blackjack II to full power and race the lightning itself.

Aria has no formal development process, no classes or tutors. The Queen of Currents has recorded a lot more about Aria than the Queen of Spades ever will; she doesn’t teach by lecture, she teaches by experience. The Aria Signet comes from spending time with the Queen—playing games or sports, racing each other, capturing flags, telling stories, telling jokes, living it up – as you fall into the Queen’s groove, the abilities of Aria come naturally to you.

![[Signet.base#Aria]]


## Fuoco
Fuoco is the Signet of the Queen of Swords, and like her it’s simple and straightforward: fire. Fire, the heat fire brings, the illumination fire sheds, and the explosions fire causes when focused and directed; fire gives us warmth and light and danger and is impossible to ignore. Fire doesn’t mess around, fire neither hems nor haws nor dillies nor dallies. Just like the Queen of Swords, fire just goes for it, as hard as it can, growing in size and power as long as there is something to consume. Fire, like passion, can be a destructive force if left completely unguided. A life without fire, like a life without passion, is cold and lonely and miserable and short.

Fuoco is taught and refined through experience (though not as informally as Aria). The Queen of Swords or one of her disciples can tell you the basics of how it works, but the only way to be sure you’ve learned it is by trying it out. The Dreamlands offer endless opportunities to do so, riding with the Queen on the back of Miguel, smiting evil and righting wrongs and finding love.

![[Signet.base#Fuoco]]


## Legno
Legno is the Signet of the Queen of Flowers, the path of nature. The path of growth, blossoming, flourishing, of life itself; not just a metaphor or symbol, Legno allows the Queen of Flowers and her followers to directly interact with what they value and study. Though not all Princesses of Flowers work with nature in the sense of animals and plants, all do work with the “nature” of things themselves. They think about whatever “ecosystem” they occupy, and they seek to use the qualities of the natural world: resilience, growth, becoming or creating the solution to your problems.

The Queen of Flowers teaches Legno directly, without saying a word. Students of the Signet are left alone in the forest of Wen-Mung, to meditate or to observe their surroundings until they arrive at a revelation by themselves… but the Queen always picks out a spot for them, finding or creating a special place where they will flourish and find what they seek.

![[Signet.base#Legno]]


## Terra
Terra is the Signet of the Queen of Jewels, tied to earth, rock, crystal, and gemstone. Earth is heavy and reliable, stone is the fundamental basis of what we build together, and gems are rewards offered by the Earth that let us grow in wealth and show off what we have earned. Of course, no other element could suit the Queen of Jewels. With Terra, her Princesses build, reinforce, protect, and exalt; they can create things to be proud of.

Princesses of Jewels learn Terra at the Queen’s feet, but never alone. There’s always a full class, learning with you—in fact, it’s impossible to solve any problems she presents or learn any insight she has to offer by yourself. Only by collaborating with others can you gain the knowledge of Terra.

![[Signet.base#Terra]]

## Lacrima
Lacrima is the Signet of the Queen of Tears, the magical teachings and techniques of Alhambra. Like the City itself, it harnesses sorrow and shadow as fuel. Its blessings often require Belief loss, or are only in effect at low Belief levels, but they help Ravens hold on to those last few scraps of belief even harder in the face of this grim, bleak world.

Unlike other Signets, Lacrima is taught formally by a structured education system; Alhambran agents are trained in their techniques before being sent upon the Bleak Harvest, though how much they learn is up to them. The Queen of Tears herself is still involved in the training—but to be in her presence for so long, talk to her for such extended periods, might destroy anyone training to be her servant. Some are crushed just by the proximity to the burden of sorrow the Queen bears. Some feel so deeply for her they never, ever wish to leave her presence. It’s better for everyone if nobody becomes too close to her.

Lacrima is by far the most developed and well-understood Signet. It’s had the longest time for people to practice it, and the most practitioners talking to each other about how they use it and how to improve it. In addition to the choice of benefit, each dot of Lacrima also comes with another bonus benefit that allows the Raven to serve the Bleak Harvest and often refill her own resources.

![[Signet.base#Lacrima]]

Lacrima behaves kind of weirdly due to its [[Raven Key]] merit.
### Lacrima Tiers
1. At this tier, the Raven feeds a burst of energy to Alhambra whenever her actions directly or indirectly cause a Breaking Point roll due to sorrow, hopelessness, or despair. Whether or not the roll succeeds, a burst of stolen hope keeps the lanterns lit, and the small portion she captures for herself grants Wisps equal to her Inner Light.
2. At this tier, the Raven can escape into shadow to fight another day. When she ends her Transformation, she can teleport to a location shrouded in shadows or darkness. The maximum range on this teleport is 100 meters, minus 10 meters for every dot of Belief she has. Dual Identity protects her from magical tracking or identification, but not mundane observation.
3. At this tier, the Raven is able to feed a trickle of energy from a harnessed Nightscape to Alhambra. When in a subverted Nightscape, she may set the Nightscape’s maximum Essence to half of its normal capacity. The Nightmares within keep trying to fill it up to its normal capacity, and the excess bleeds out, turning into Wisps sent off to keep the lanterns lit; if she is present when excess Essence is bled off, she can add it to her own Wisps instead.
4. At this tier, the Raven harnesses an astonishing burst of energy when her actions directly or indirectly cause a Breaking Point due to sorrow, despair, or hopelessness and that roll fails. The energy is sent to keep the lanterns lit, but the small portion she is able to capture for herself gives her Wisps equal to her normal maximum, which can put her above her maximum if she already has some Wisps in reserve. She can’t regain any more Wisps until she spends enough to get her below her usual maximum.
5. At this tier, the Raven has utterly devoted herself to the Last City and the Last Queen. When her Belief is 3 or less, and she is Putting It All On The Line, her total Inner Light is doubled.
## Tempestra
Tempesta is the Signet of the Queen of Storms, the Signet of pure destruction. She’s forgotten whatever element she was once attuned to, if she ever had one. Fire provides light and heat and warmth and civilization. Lightning powers our technology and grants insight. Even blood sustains our lives. Acid corrodes, and unmakes, and dissolves, and destroys. It purifies—when everything else melts away, only the pure core remains. She’s never put any thought into the symbolism of her Signet, of course.; it’s just what’s right. Tempesta asks you to destroy yourself and empowers you to destroy what is around you, to purify through annihilation.

Nobody teaches Tempesta. Like everything else from the Queen of Storms, this knowledge comes from one thing: her ceaseless violence. When transported in dreams, her Princesses see the swift, expert, brutal carnage she wreaks against the Darkness. To learn its higher tiers, they travel to her side and participate in her crusade themselves. Even other Princesses admit that the violence of the Queen of Storms is beautiful to behold. For the Princesses of Storms, the display sticks with them, echoes in their mind’s eye, where they watch it again and again and see one move flow into the other, one kill chain into another, and see in it a mathematical pattern of such purity and beauty it should be drawn across the universe.

![[Signet.base#Tempestra]]

## Specchio
Specchio, the Signet of the Queen of Mirrors, is the Signet of reflection and distortion, images and fractures, of vanity and denial. Much like everything about the Queen of Mirrors, she won’t acknowledge hers is the Signet of denial and distortion. She doesn’t even realize it, and many of her Princesses don’t either. To them it is the Signet of glory, exaltation, and the self. The one True Signet for the True Heir, unique to themselves and superior to all others. The only magic capable of adequately reflecting their glory and the only magic capable of helping them bear the crushing weight of being the world’s only savior.

The Queen of Mirrors doesn’t teach you her Signet. She talks with you through the mirror, in reality and in dreams, and says it’s YOU teaching HER, that she is only bringing out what you already have and were destined to have all along. All the other Queens would be envious of how effectively she’s able to bring out someone’s potential, if not for the fact that the Queen of Mirrors genuinely does not believe she knows her own Signet and genuinely believes that the True Heir already knew all of it and only needs to be reminded.
