The territory where [[the Noble]] would muster a counterattack against the [[Darkness]] was known as [[Campaign- City of Sin/Locations/The Dreamlands & Dark World/The Dreamlands]]. Opinions are divided on whether the Dreamlands existed at all before the [[The Darkness and the Cataclysm|Cataclysm]]. Were they always there as a tangible manifestation of our ability to dream, or were they created by escaping Nobles as a safe place to hold out and plan? The Dreamlands may or may not be where our dreams come from, but it is certainly where they go to. Unbounded by space or time, all that we imagine gathers there. Ideas there can take on existence of their own, living just as we do.

The Noble would not have been satisfied with this potential paradise, because they knew aspiration meant nothing without the will to see it done in reality. Hope is not empty pining, hope is the will to make a better tomorrow come about. They always meant to regroup and save the rest of the world.

Yet they were betrayed and entrapped in the Dreamlands. The Light of Hope was reflected back upon them and they were swept into the narrative of the dream, offered a place where they had already won back all they fought for. Their Kingdoms were rebuilt not by thought and effort but simply by the wish that they be so. All the Noble could see was what they wanted to see; a flourishing home for humankind.

Instead of striking back to cast off the Darkness and save the suffering, their leaders, now known as [[The Queens|the Radiant]], slumbered in unreality. Not knowing that anyone needed their help, they played out the best of all possible worlds as an impossible fantasy. Happy, complacent, carried on by nothing but wishful thinking.

[[The Dark Queens]] knew that the Light would build again. At least, they must have been the ones to figure that out, because the Darkness is incapable of those thoughts. The Darkness wanted only to consume and torture and ruin,
and was finding itself in a world with nothing left to despoil. It couldn’t be content in success, desiring to endlessly repeat itself. To do that, the world would be redone, skewed from the beginning in the Darkness’s favor.

The Dark Queens found creatures called [[The Wardens]], bizarre beings made up of disjointed and discordant stories and ideas. They enlisted these Wardens to monitor and maintain the Trap and keep the Radiant dreaming. With them out of the way, the Darkness would have an endless
buffet of helpless humans to inflict suffering on.

The world was started anew. Nobody is certain  how, nor does it matter. It was begun again with the taint of the Darkness, the taint of fear, drenching it down to its very foundations. Everything that was good was twisted to its worst, most painful, most fearful incarnation. Sources of comfort became sources of terror; every friend, a potential enemy. Humanity restarted, suffocating under the thick shroud of Darkness, with its most dedicated leaders and protectors entrapped far away. The previous incarnation of reality withered into the Dark World, a dead and lightless place hanging off the new world like a diseased appendix. The Darkness was turned loose to feed, held from overfeeding only by Humanity’s barest, guttering
glimpses of the Light. Our aspirations and hope would be stunted, slow, and each new discovery would bring with it a new form of fear and misery. With the Noble entrapped, the people’s lives would be a trap as well, one they couldn’t
conceive of escaping.

The history of humanity would be rewritten from page one as a chronicle of darkness.
