# Sensitivity

It isn’t just her heroic character that drives a Princess to fight the Darkness: The strength of her Inner Light pushes against the Darkness, and the Darkness pushes back, building up a pressure inside her. She’s better able to see the Darkness of the world to fight against it.

Unfortunately, even for those who are specially attuned to detect the Darkness, the fact that Darkness underlies the world makes it extremely hard to pin down. Since there’s such an overwhelming amount of Darkness as “back-
ground radiation,” Sensitivity can’t really provide a compass and waypoint to sources of problems. It just lets you know something is wrong—whether you want it to or not.

# Acute Sensitivity

Princesses are able to detect major spikes in Pure Darkness at the moment they occur, such as the opening of a Dark Gate, an attack by Nightmares or Darkspawn. especially severe Integrity breaking points, or major use of powers
granted by Pure Darkness. This might manifest as pain, a sense of urgency, panic, or impending doom, but it’s never pleasant. It also doesn’t tell you WHERE the problem is, only that it exists somewhere in your radius and its general direction. The first attack of Acute Sensitivity is too often what spurs a nakama of Princesses into action, not only to save the one endangered but also to stop it from happening again.

The stronger the Potency of whatever pinged Acute Sensitivity, the stronger the feeling of Acute Sensitivity and the further away it activates. The exception is that if the base (Potency (unmodified by Tension Conditions or other buffs) of the effect is 3 or more dots higher than the Princess’s Inner Light, it doesn’t ping her Sensitivity at all. The surge is too big. You can’t pick it out from the rest of the
world, it would be like trying to take a picture of an entire city while you’re standing inside one building.

You can’t bear the pain of the entire world, you can’t even hold it all in your mind. Most Princesses are quietly grateful that this stops them from being aware of problems that are far above their “pay grade” and would be certain
to kill them. That’s because the Princesses who aren’t quietly grateful for this seek out problems far above their “pay grade” and get killed.

# Chronic Sensitivity

The pressure of the Darkness pushing back against your Inner Light can be draining, exhausting. It wears on you, it wears you down, and any way to hide or build shelter from it won’t last. It comes from every direction, and the brighter your Inner Light, the more the Darkness tries to extinguish it.

When the Darkness pushes on you too hard, or for too long, it wears you down. You feel worse, physically and emotionally. Positive emotions feel numb while negative emotions feel intense. You’re exhausted but you can’t sleep. The Light of Hope inside you feels weaker and the pressure from the Darkness builds up from all directions.

While you’re near an ongoing font of Pure Darkness—an active Nightscape, a Bleed or Dark Gate through which people are being dragged, a loose Darkspawn in the real world, a Cataphract, repeated patterns of Integrity breaking points, something that both spreads the taint of the Darkness as fear and is empowered by it—and haven’t been making progress against it, you roll your Inner Light dots at a fixed interval. If the base, unbuffed Potency of the source of Darkness is equal to your Inner Light you roll every 3 days, if it’s within one point you roll every week, and if it’s within two points you roll every two weeks. If it’s further off than that, you don’t roll. You also may be forced to make
this roll when confronted with absolutely overwhelming suffering wrought by Darkness, even if they weren’t sufficiently your fault to trigger a Belief Compromise.

When you get more successes on this roll than your current stacks of the [[Shadows]] Condition (p. 271), or if you choose to skip the roll, you get another stack of Shadows. (The Storyteller might choose to make the same roll for everyone, just so the group doesn’t get wildly out of sync.) If you don’t, you get +1 to all subsequent Sensitivity rolls until you make progress or gain a stack of Shadows.

The good news is, as someone native to this reality, there is a baseline level of Darkness you can tolerate: one stack of Shadows. The existence of fear itself won’t debilitate you, and the presence of the evils you are meant to fight
doesn’t immediately weaken you against them. It will cause, at most, intermittent headaches or nausea or psychosomatic symptoms. The first stack of Shadows comes quickly when there’s a font of Darkness, but that’s the level you can handle, and as long as you make progress fighting it in a timely manner, you’re letting off the pressure faster than it can build up.

Each stack of Shadows past the first is way, way more than you can handle. Each one lowers your effective Inner Light by 1, to a minimum of 1. The only way to resolve these Conditions is to make progress against the source of Darkness that inflicted them, or to get lucky and have it resolved by someone else. A victory against the force of Darkness that caused the Sensitivity hit removes one stack of Shadows. Defeating the source conclusively removes all stacks. Either way, you don’t get Beats for resolving them.

Chronic Sensitivity is, unfortunately, also the only sense a Princess can use to narrow down the source of Darkness. Each time you gain a stack of Shadows, your pain and discomfort and
emotional exhaustion gets worse, and you receive some clue about where the source of the Darkness is and how you have to locate or fight it. These clues may take the form of shocking, haunting visions from which a few bits of information can be divined, coincidental encounters with those affected, or even opportunistic attacks by Darkspawn.

It’s, at the very best, a mixed blessing. If you’re flummoxed and out of options, then it can draw you to where you need to be; but may very well leave you too weakened to fight it once you get there. And if you try to abdicate, to leave the War for Hope, you’ll find the War won’t leave you.
