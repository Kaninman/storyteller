# Shikigami

Shikigami are the helpful familiar spirits sent from the Dreamlands to aid the Radiant in their quest, and almost every Radiant nakama will have one. A Shikigami is a separate entity that helps the entire group as a common resource. It has its own modified character sheet and traits, and its own unique capabilities. The Shikigami Merit is the special Loyalty Merit for all Radiant Princesses, and unlike other shared group Merits like Changeling’s Hollow or Werewolf’s Totem, they do not scale directly with the amount of dots the group as a whole has invested into them: the Shikigami Merit is a binary: “do you have access to a Shikigami or do you not have access to a Shikigami?” If you have one, then more characters with the Shikigami Merit do make the Shikigami stronger indirectly by giving it more resources or skills to call on. Members of a nakama who do not have the Shikigami Merit for some reason (such as if they are Twilights in a tenuous alliance, or they come with their own history where their Shikigami died) don’t get the benefits of and can’t donate power to the Shikigami no matter how much they interact; they need to buy the Merit to represent a bond of trust between them.

A Shikigami is a facilitator and sidekick, not a main char- acter who directly solves problems. Shikigami don’t have a lot of direct magical power on their own, but they have support abilities, can channel and be empowered by their Princesses, are very inconspicuous, and can make Princess’s lives much, much, much easier. A Shikigami will monitor the local area for developing trouble spots and leads on the forces of Darkness, coordinate the group together when needed, open the most convenient pas- sages to the Dreamlands, receive communication from the Queens, maintain secret identities, do chores, bother the group about holding up to their ideals, and provide a mentor figure to look up to; they try to be taken very seriously, and usually aren’t.

To be frank, one of the most useful aspects of a Shikigami is that they have absolutely nothing to do all day and looooots of time on their hands.

# Playing Shikigami

Shikigami are communally-run Storyteller characters who make the lives of the players and the Storyteller easier. The Storyteller is the arbiter of what the shikigami can do, but the group determines the shikigami’s personality. The Storyteller gets across the basics of what the shikigami can present to the group, and then players can all offer suggestions at any point in the scene for what the shikigami does and how, playing up their Drive and Dread, or merely adding to a scene and reinforcing the Shikigami’s personality and nature.

For the Storyteller, the Shikigami is an easy conduit of exposition: they bring the group leads on evil activity or relevant lore from the Fearless Kingdoms to spur the group to action. Shikigami also cover things that realistically need to be covered but you don’t want to spend a whole lot of time depicting because they’d become repetitive, like maintaining a Princess’s secret identity.

For the players, a Shikigami is like a communal familiar that they can channel spells and skills through to accomplish things at a remove. Shikigami aren’t supposed to overshadow player characters, so the players will lend their own Skills and Charms and other abilities to the Shikigami; but the Shikigami can use them in a context that the player character couldn’t or doesn’t want to. Any task that would require someone to stay out of the action, for example, can be done by the Shikigami with the players’ skills, and none of the players have to sit out a fun scene.

For the Shikigami to use a Skill or Charm, a player must channel it: the player bestowing the Shikigami with their ability pays an additional Wisp to lend that power long enough for one roll. This could represent calling on training and guidance the player character gave the Shikigami in the past, or a direct psychic link through which the character beseeches the Shikigami to Do Your Best. Shikigami can only use Charms that both they and the Princess channeling into them have, and the channeler can add their own Upgrades. They can also have up to two Upgrades to add to the channeler’s Upgrades without the channeler knowing them.

# Origin

Shikigami are physical beings that used to be ephemeral beings. Despite their physical bodies, they still have Power, Finesse, and Resistance Attributes, just to abbreviate their capabilities. They have Wisps instead of Essence, they don’t rely on Manifestation Conditions, can’t be exorcized or abjured, and shine the Light of Hope. Shikigami are volunteers sent from the Dreamlands by the Queens to locate, advise, and guide Princesses in their missions. Shikigami would love it if this was a matter of being chosen for a great honor by their Queen, having the title ritually bestowed on them, and heading out to the real world where they select an appropriate form to best provide guidance but hide as an innocuous object or animal.

It’s not, though.

The entire process is a hacked-together, jury-rigged mess. None of the magical “systems” of the world are set up to facilitate this. To even get to the real world with a portion of their memories and identity intact, the Queens basically have to kill them in a special way that reincarnates them directly into a ghost native to the real world, skipping the part where they were alive; they can hold on to bits and pieces of their Dreamlands identity the same way a ghost can hold on to bits and pieces of its former life. Then they appear as an ephemeral entity, on a strange and distorted “frequency” of Twilight that allows them to see and interact with ghosts and spirits as if through a fuzzy, lossy connection.

A Shikigami takes a physical vessel by using a variant of the Claim manifestation that allows complete and perfect melding with an inanimate object or nonhuman animal of Size 3 or less. But due to the lossy, hacked-together nature of the Shikigami’s ephemeral form, a Shikigami doesn’t have free reign over selecting its vessel —only certain ones will work, according to a set of criteria that barely makes any sense to Shikigami themselves, much less anyone else. The process also requires them to enlist the help of nearby minor ghosts and spirits, who they have to bribe with Wisps, which are comparable to the artificial aspartame sweetener version of Essence. These ghosts and spirits retain a weak link to the Shikigami as the Wisps swirl in their ephemeral Corpus, allowing the Shikigami to contact them later… for as much as it’s worth to have ephemeral acquaintances. These ephemerals are known as “minor Shikigami,” but are much less helpful or devoted
than the regular kind.

The process of Claiming a vessel results in a Shikigami as described in these rules, not the usual process of Claiming as described in the CofD rulebook. The resulting entity isn’t considered to be under the Claim Manifestation Condition, it just IS the resulting Shikigami. If it dies, it has to seek out a new vessel, and do it quickly; the nakama will need to help.

Claiming causes the Shikigami to take on some traits of the object or animal it uses as a vessel, tying it up with the object’s Resonance or merging its nature with the animal. Even merging entirely with an animal is not a significant shift in identity, because a human persona just has a lot more TO it, but a stuffed animal shikigami might become uncomfortably huggy, and a dog shikigami might form elaborate strategies to assault and destroy the dreaded vacuum cleaner.

# Making a Shikigami

The shikigami is created communally by the troupe. It’s a support character both in a mechanical and storytelling sense, usually played by the Storyteller but able to be “controlled” by any player in order to directly help the group or contribute to a scene. The shikigami interacts with individual characters of the nakama, as well as with the nakama’s character as a whole. You want a character who syncs with the group, but not perfectly—a minor conflict that informs but doesn’t interfere with the working relationship is a great way to let your characters express who they are by their reactions to it. It can also be another knob to control how comedic the game is. A funny shikigami in a serious Chronicle serves as comic relief and a serious Shikigami in a more comedic Chronicle serves to ground the antics and remind everyone what they truly fight for.

## Drive or Dread

Like a Princess character, a Shikigami has a Drive and a Dread, and like a Princess character, you need to determine what these are first, before you even know what form the Shikigami takes. Their form is going to either support or contrast with their Drive and Dread. A Shikigami’s Drive and Dread are not quite the same as a Princess’s though; they are support characters both in game terms and in the game world, and they know it. It’s what they signed on for. One choice will be “group-driven,” devoted to how they interact with the nakama, and primarily expressed in how the nakama acts and not themselves. A Shikigami’s group-driven Drive is the principle, idea or value they want the nakama to embody and triumph through

Validation doesn’t come from personal triumph, it’s when the group triumphs under their guidance. Their group-driven Dread is usually something they want the nakama to avoid: a pitfall that too many have fallen into, an error they know they made themselves, a fate they can’t stand the thought of, or the thing that just bothers them the most.

A Shikigami’s group-driven Drive or Dread provides the most benefit when there’s a minor conflict or misalignment between them and the nature of the nakama. Whatever their Drive is, it should be something that is in line with the nakama’s values but does not come completely naturally or easily. It’s an ideal they have to live up to, and takes effort to do so. Their Dread should be something the group falls into easily. An uptight Shikigami who wants everyone to be diligent needs at least a few Princesses who want to take it easy. A Shikigami who prizes unity above all needs to be around a nakama who frequently fight with each other.

When the Shikigami’s Drive or Dread conflicts with what a member of the nakama wants to do, that member has a choice. If she goes along with the Shikigami’s desires, she restores one Willpower from the relief and reassurance of going along with a mentor figure; then if the course of action succeeds, the Shikigami regains all of its Willpower. If she goes against the Shikigami’s advice, at significant risk, and is correct to do so, she restores all her Willpower. She doesn’t just get the Willpower from defying the Shikigami, it has to turn out that she made the right call and the result was better than what the Shikigami would have asked for. She doesn’t get the Willpower if the Shikigami is obviously wrong right out the gate, that’s not a milestone of personal development and there was no personal risk involved in going against it.

- Choose a Drive or Dread for the group’s Shikigami. It should conflict with the group’s overall approach, but only in a minor way. The other will be picked in a minute.

## Form

Now that you know what the shikigami is about, you need to know what it looks like, and how that form plays into their Drive and Dread. It could facilitate their role, or it could hinder it, but it should interact with their personality, Drive and Dread.

Shikigami can incarnate as an object or non-human animal of up to Size 3, though an object needs to have some kind of articulation that the shikigami can use to manipulate his environment. Toys with a general humanoid or at least quadrupedal shape like stuffed animals, dolls, and action figures are the most common. A Shikigami incarnated into an inanimate object is kiiiiiiiiind of like a living thing, but only kind of—if it occupies an object that looks like a living thing, it will have a psychological need to fulfill the physical needs of that thing, like eating, getting water, or napping in sunbeams.

So what IS it? What it looks like will inform what it’s able to do and where it’s able to hide, but it’s more than that. Whatever the shikigami incarnates into, it’s going to take on SOME traits associated with it. It’s going to be the group’s mascot, a symbol of your association. There are “silly” or “serious” forms a shikigami can take—a stuffed bear is sillier than a cat, which is sillier than a computer system. You can adapt anything on that scale to the tone of your game, but you have to be aware of how you’re doing it, whether you’re playing the type straight or playing it for contrast. For a more serious Chronicle, a computer is a detached and professional Mission Control network system, while the stuffed bear’s struggle against the limitations of his form symbolize the desperate nature of the nakama’s struggle to fight the Darkness with limited help.

In a comedic Chronicle, the stuffed bear gets into wacky antics, while the computer tries to be a detached and professional Mission Control and is constantly exasperated at the dumb bullshit his nakama keeps getting into. In the previous step, you aimed your choice of either the Shikigami’s Drive or Dread at how they interact with the group. Now, choose the other more like a personality trait
or quirk all their own: something they’re About that’s particular to their personality and how it interacts with their new form. This Drive or Dread works just like the version Princesses have in terms of how it restores Willpower to the Shikigami.

A Shikigami’s personal Drive or Dread could be a funny distraction, a source of bias or blind spots, or a tragic reminder of what they sacrificed to be able to help the nakama.

A Shikigami of a rabbit may have a Dread of larger predators, because the animal vessel has a fear of predators and the Shikigami spirit was shaken by having no choice but to possess and reanimate a freshly-eviscerated rabbit as a vessel.

A Shikigami made of a child’s toy with a Drive to get sweets might prepare a reward of chocolate and candy for the nakama after a job well done… and then restore all Willpower by gorging himself on the treats before the nakama even gets there, being found groaning in a pile of candy wrappers, proclaiming “I regret nothing.”

- Choose what physical form the Shikigami has for its vessel: an inanimate object or animal of Size 3 or less.
- Choose the remaining Dread or Drive to be a way in which their new vessel affects their behavior or personality.

## Attributes

Shikigami have Power, Finesse, and Resistance Attributes like ephemeral beings, even though they are physically embodied. These Attributes reflect how skilled they are in different approaches to problems, and are added to their special Shikigami Benefits or the nakama’s loaned Skills to determine their dice pools.

- Put one dot in each of Power, Finesse, and Resistance Attributes. Then assign 5 more dots among those three Attributes.

## Skills

Shikigami don’t have any Skills unless they buy an ability that says otherwise; they will usually employ Skills lent from their nakama. However, Shikigami never suffer untrained penalties for Skill rolls, they may purchase Skill Specialties without any dots in the associated skill, and they all have the Interdisciplinary Specialty Merit applied to all Skills—when channeling someone else’s Skill or their own Benefits, they can apply their own Specialties.

- Don’t assign Skills to the Shikigami, but pick three Skill Specialties. Each can be applied to any Skill roll where it would be relevant, regardless of particular Skill.

## Shikigami Benefits

While Shikigami don’t have what in the Chronicles of Darkness rule system are called “Skills,” they do have actual skills in that they are better at some things than others. These are Shikigami Benefits. These are less of an objective measure of their own knowledge and more of an assessment of what kind of things they can accomplish for the group. They usually don’t need to be rolled, they just add a benefit available to the nakama.

- Assign 7 dots among the five Advantages: Coordination, Lore, Observation, Secrecy, and Sneakiness.

## Merits

Shikigami can purchase some Merits, but only ones that make sense for a Shikigami to have. Maybe it can maintain a false identity to have Allies or Contacts over the phone and Internet, or it can create a system of pulleys and place wooden blocks on the pedals to make use of Stunt Driving. ## Advantages

Now, derive the various Advantages typical to a character, like their Speed and Health. Shikigami are weaker than most beings, and their Advantages reflect this.

Shikigami don’t have much damage capacity, because they’re small, and they’re kind of always holding their own bodies together. At least a Shikigami can’t bleed out – even if it’s in a living animal vessel, blood isn’t actually necessary for it to live in the short-term. It can hurt like absolute Bejesus but a combination of being able to heal its own vessel and the ability to function in a nonliving vessel means blood loss takes a long while to actually become a problem.

Shikigami have a Belief, Inner Light, Wisp reserve, and the ability to use Defiance like a Princess. They don’t have a Calling, Queen, or Oaths. They have Dual Identity protections for their mundane object form, but they won’t really help if someone sees them talking and running around.

- A Shikigami’s Health is its Resistance +2, regardless of its Size.
- A Shikigami’s Willpower is its Resistance + Lore.
- A Shikigami’s Defense is its Finesse + Sneakiness.
- A Shikigami’s Initiative is its Finesse + Observation.
- A Shikigami’s Speed is its Power + Coordination + Secrecy.
- A Shikigami’s Belief is 8.

## Advancing Shikigami

Shikigami grow and develop in their capabilities just like Princesses do. They only receive Luminous Beats, not normal Beats. They can earn them through their own actions, but it’s rare; most of the time they accumulate a Luminous Beat when the nakama as a whole gains a number of Luminous Beats equal to the number of members it has. Shikigami also get a Luminous Beat when one single event (like the end of a session) grants every member of the nakama a normal Beat.

A Shikigami’s own Luminous Experience can be spent on advancing its abilities according to the table below. The group has to agree to any expenditure of Experiences.

# Shikigami Benefits

Shikigami Benefits can be used in place of Skills in a pinch. When one of the Benefits applies and nobody spends a Wisp to channel their own Skill, roll the Shikigami’s Attribute + Benefit. If they’re directly carrying out a task related to that Benefit, it doesn’t cost a Wisp; if not, they can use one of their own Wisps to add it to their dice pool. Normal uses of these Benefits don’t require dice rolls; if your Shikigami has Secrecy• , they don’t have to roll to deceive whoever they call to spin a phony story, they just do it.

Shikigami Benefits are all associated with specific Charms. Increasing the Benefit allows the Shikigami to channel those Charms from their Princesses more efficiently. At one dot, the Shikigami can cast the base Charm with no Upgrades, and then gets one additional Upgrade for each dot after that. They can only use Charms and Upgrades channeled through them by their nakama. The other functions of the Shikigami Benefits work no matter what Charms their people have to channel.

## Lore

_Associated Charm: [[Restoration]]_
Shikigami have access to a source of information that no Princess could know, no matter how well-read they are about the real world: the lore of the Dreamlands and the Fearless Kingdoms, which they are able to get from the source far more easily than others. Lore is the Shikigami’s ability to gather, coordinate, and recall that information for the nakama’s use. ^lore

(-): The Shikigami knows the basics about the Fearless Kingdoms and the Queens, the kind of stuff that’s covered in this book’s “setting” chapter. ^lore0

•: The Shikigami talks to the Queens enough to regularly ferry messages and progress reports about the nakama. Once per chapter per dot, a member of the nakama can benefit from their Queen Mentor without taking the time to go to the Dreamlands and speak in depth, thanks to an appropriate message and response that takes about an hour in total to relay. ^lore1

••: The Shikigami is now well-traveled enough that it’s able to provide enough information to count as a 2-dot Library for any and all Skills on the subjects of the Fearless Kingdoms, the Dreamlands, Nightscapes, and Darkspawn, allowing research that would otherwise be impossible. Each roll using the Library takes about four hours as the Shikigami goes back and forth between the Princess asking questions and consulting with its information sources. ^lore2

•••: The Shikigami is proactive and well-traveled enough that it can provide the previous two benefits without taking extra time. Even if it can’t put the research information together to draw a conclusion on its own, it has good enough recall of facts that it doesn’t have to go back and check sources, and it’s got enough foresight into what problems might come up that it’s got a missive from a Queen that’s suited for this particular situation. It also gains enough expertise to serve as a reference source for anything involving its Skill Specialties. ^lore3

## Coordination

_Associated Charm: [[Conjure]]_
Shikigami can serve as organizers, logistics officers, and even secretaries to their nakama, and their Coordination measures how well they keep things running smoothly. As Princesses need to live their normal lives in addition to their magical ones, they may be split up all over the place when danger arrives, or they may not be easily able to reach each other; and they will always end up in situations where they need to talk to each other quickly without warning. ^coordination

(-): The Shikigami has a cell phone and everyone’s cell phone numbers. ^coordination0

•: The Shikigami has a method of asynchronous communication for the group that cannot be traced, decrypted, or decoded, like the world’s most secure group chat. No matter the circumstance, the nakama can know things other members of the nakama found out in previous scenes (though not scenes that happen simultaneously), and know when another nakama member needs something that relates to whatever they’re presently dealing with. ^coordination1

••: The Shikigami maintains a method of real-time always-on group communication, like those cool miniature headset microphones. You don’t need to specifically “take a call” to use this, you can freely talk as if any member of the nakama was in the room. Nobody can hear it when members talk to you, and the communication is uncrackable and untraceable by electronic or supernatural means, but someone close to you can just, you know, listen to hear you talking. ^coordination2

•••: The Shikigami maintains a method of real-time always-on covert group communication, such as a telepathic network, or a Codec that transmits sound by reading subtle movements in your throat muscles when you speak inaudibly and plays it to others by vibrating the little bones in your ear. ^coordination3

## Observation

_Associated Charm: [[Detect]]_
Since shikigami have oodles of free time on their hands, they often engage in scouting and reconnaissance operations in the area the nakama operates, and can alert them of trouble brewing. This also frees up the nakama to live their normal lives rather than devote free time to scouting the area when they’d be much worse at it. This Benefit represents their ability to gather information from the world by observation. ^observation

(-): The Shikigami has all day to stay inside and watch TV, browse the Internet, read newspapers, and get information any other way someone staying at home can do so. It will be generally aware enough of sources of Darkness and potential Blossomings that it can provide the information to start an adventure based on locating these things, but without any additional details. ^observation0

•: The Shikigami can get outside, and has all day to watch a specific area you designate, so long as there’s a way to remain hidden and inconspicuous. They’ll see anyone going in and out, what activities go on, anything an observer would notice when that observer has all day and is inconspicuous. ^observation1

••: The Shikigami can now follow individuals around even if they’re moving, or can locate and then observe areas based on criteria: “Try to find any new, suspicious flower stands that pop up” and the like. ^observation2

•••: Now the Shikigami has access to some “minor Shikigami” acquaintances to carry out the abilities of the previous two Tiers in multiple places at once. Don’t give the minor Shikigami orders that are too complicated or require too much discretion. They don’t “get” a lot of things. ^observation3

## Sneakiness

_Associated Charm: [[Force]]_
Acting on or in the real world is always difficult for a Shikigami, because their powers are weak and aimed at support; but often that support is invaluable. The Sneakiness Benefit covers both the ability to escape notice and the ability to manipulate the environment, because to a Shikigami, they’ll always be linked. ^sneakiness

(-): The Shikigami can mess up your room, and probably will, but that’s about it. ^sneakiness0

•: The Shikigami can swipe unattended items and fiddle with unattended objects, so long as you can bring them nearby and there’s ample time to get in and out without being observed. ^sneakiness1

••: The Shikigami can sneak into places on its own, without you bringing them close by, but it still can’t work with the risk of being caught: despite being a small inconspicuous object or animal, it’s as difficult to hide its actions as a full-sized human. ^sneakiness2

•••: The Shikigami can now work WITH the risk of being caught, working with stealth when someone is in the room with them, able to fully employ the stealth benefits of being small and inconspicuous. ^sneakiness3

## Secrecy

_Associated Charm: [[Illusion]]_
One of, perhaps THE, most valuable functions of a Shikigami is keeping the secret identities of the nakama secret. Though Dual Identity protects against direct and immediate investigation, a lot of sudden absences from her normal responsibilities—especially if she’s in school—will quickly tip others off that Something is Up with the Princess. Secrecy is the Shikigami’s ability to cover that up. Though they shouldn’t directly interact with people in person, Shikigami can use phones, (even if they might have to tape a pen to their stuffed-animal arms to be able to hit the number pad), they can post on social media, they can forge notes and verify cover stories. ^secrecy

(-): The Shikigami does its best to forge handwriting and make a few calls with fake voices to come up with a plausible cover story for why characters will be absent, if it has a
day or so of warning. ^secrecy0

•: With minor illusions, quick thinking, and some clever use of props, the Shikigami can now fabricate a reason why characters will be absent with only a few minutes’ warning. ^secrecy1

••: With slightly less minor illusions, decoys, skillful puppetry, or an easily swappable mannequin vessel, the Shikigami can also appear to be one of the characters and play them in their normal life. (They don’t have to choose ahead of time what member they can appear as, and they can only be one member at a time.) The Shikigami can’t perfectly emulate the character, they can’t do very difficult tasks and friends will report the character was acting kind of weird, but it’s nothing TOO suspicious. ^secrecy2

•••: Enlisting the help of its ephemeral acquaintances, the Shikigami can emulate multiple group members at once. Group members emulated by these minor Shikigami are, uh… they’re going to be acting pretty strangely, but nothing you can’t explain away with heat stroke or food poisoning. ^secrecy3
