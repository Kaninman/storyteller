# The State of the World

Other creatures of the dark have secret societies and hierarchies, or at least traditions to follow. [[The Queens|the Radiant]] do not. They don’t have a system for who is in charge of this city or that city or how to resolve disputes, they don’t have any sort of formal community in the real world. The first Blossoming—the first time someone awakened fully to the power of the Light of Hope and realized their destiny—of someone sent by the Queens to be reborn was in 1992. There weren’t many of them, but their numbers grew over time, and they made an impact on the world. While still rare, Blossoming Princesses tend to now awaken in clusters to seek each other out rather than as isolated individuals unable to face their duty alone.

The first generation of Princesses is growing up, and some few have even attained positions of power or influence. But they are too few, far too few to rely upon. You can speak to the Queens, seek their guidance, eve[[CORE RULES/INFRASTRUCTURE/System Packs/Princess the Hopeful-Crystal/Lore/Setting Lore/The Dreamlands]]/Princess the Hopeful-Crystal/Lore/Setting Lore/The Dreamlands]]. You can meet thousands and thousands of other Princesses and subjects, and have no idea which of them, if any, actually live in the real world.

The Radiant could try and come together worldwide. It’s not a new concept; it’s known as the “Kingdom Come.” In theory, with the Internet, it wouldn’t even be that hard if everyone came out and spoke up at once. Then the Radiant would be able to apply all that they have in the best possible way to save the world. And if anything went wrong in the process of making the Kingdom Come, if their efforts were discovered and undermined, or if the Radiant weren’t yet up to the challenge, then they’d be slaughtered at the moment they were needed most and would never again have a chance. Is the fact they still hide proof that the Darkness holds power over them, or is it just common sense?

In this day, there’s just one thing a Princess can truly rely upon: her nakama. A Japanese term meaning “a group of friends whose common experiences made them closer than family,” your nakama are your steadfast friends and allies. You’ll survive with them, and then live your life with them. True friends are the ultimate power.

But while the Radiant don’t have resources and secret societies, [[The Twilight Queens|the Twilight]] do. [[Campaign- City of Sin/Locations/The Dreamlands & Dark World/Twilight Kingdoms/Alhambra|Alhambra]] has the intelligence, the organization, the maps, the library, all the knowledge that the Princesses of Tears have accumulated over lifetimes of stealing hope from the world. Armies of loyal soldiers are
being raised by the Princesses of Storms, cells of people illuminated by their sickly light and caustic hatred. Throngs of loyal followers gazing up in adoration are enraptured by the Princesses of Mirrors, believing each one to be the perfect True Heir.

These dark magical girls can be allies… at least, until they are inevitably forced to cross purposes.

# The War for Hope

Today, Princesses are fast approaching the tipping point that will determine if they can save the world or if their efforts will be crushed by backlash. The taint of fear suffuses our hearts and our reality, and no matter how much we wish we can never be utterly rid of it. But we knew we’d never be free of hatred or sadness either, and we made something wonderful despite them. It won’t be easy, and it might not be possible, but t[[CORE RULES/INFRASTRUCTURE/System Packs/Princess the Hopeful-Crystal/Mechanics/Nightscapes|Nightscapes]]ystem Packs/Princess the Hopeful-Crystal/Mechanics/Nightscapes|Nightscapes]]. Just as a Noble will Blossom when the love surging in her is so great it can’t be contained by her body, the fear and anguish in the human heart can explode outward into a Nightscape, a trapped miniature realm of dark dreams where its involuntary creator is both the ruler and the tortured prisoner—and from which beings of fear are birthed and escape into the world, compulsively re-enacting their own creation, spreading fear further and further. Other occult powers of the world catch glimpses of this, but are so immersed in [[Darkness|the Darkness]] that they think this is simply a natural thing that happens. The Noble know better. They don’t know how many Nightscapes there are, or how many new ones flourish each day – but they know they need to be stopped, their predations warded off, their injuries undone, and their creators freed.

The Dreamlands, the Dark World, and even this world hold lost lore of the Fearless Kingdoms that could mean the difference between victory and defeat. What techniques have been forgotten? Are there ancient alliances that could be called upon again? What do we know of the foes that face us? Who came before us, and what did they accomplish, and what can we finish? Are there any more Kings or Queens, caught and still swept in the Gales? What denizens of the Dreamlands can be convinced to join our cause, and what can they offer?

And a Princess knows that she will have to be a leader. That above the immediate fights and the quests she is going to have to unite people in belief in themselves and belief in her. Princesses today are capable of Transforming into their “perfected selves,” a different identity of a different person who is what they could have been. It lets them hide their actions and maintain their normal lives, but it didn’t exist in the Fearless Kingdom. Eventually, she’s going to have to fully assume that perfected self, cast off the pretense of transformation, declare her true identity to the world and face its scorn and judgment and opposition.

The Darkness may not be able to create or aspire, but people can. In a world blinded and blanketed by fear, it is also a powerful tool. Darkness demands people give in to it, lash out to make the fear go away for a moment, but there are those who see the Darkness and the Nightscapes it creates as means to power. If they inflict the Darkness upon others they will be spared. Whether or not they are aware of the source of their power, they are enriched by Darkness and do not take kindly to anyone trying to take that from them. Others, lost entirely to fear and hollowed out utterly, become unfeeling conduits of terror into the world. Princesses who cannot stand the pressure and shatter their Belief yet still cling to life and long for power become the Enthroned, shrieking parodies of what humanity can be who embrace their own Nightscapes in order to have a realm to rule and punish others within. The world is full of awful beings, eldritch horrors, callous monstrosity, all from a thousand disparate origins but all coming from the same thing: the taint of the Darkness, the taint of fear.

Princesses need to gather together, seek allies, uplift each other and the people they protect. They must convince those they can that their cause isn’t just right, but possible; then thwart those they can’t, who outnumber them greatly. They need to unravel the mysteries of a world corrupted to the bone with something that should have never existed. The Queens can offer them mentorship and guidance and love but they cannot solve problems; it’s Princesses who must take action to fight evil . Against this, to have any hope of success, a Princess will need power. She will need to grow and mature into someone who can handle this burden. She will need to believe in herself and her ability to change the world even when it all seems hopeless. She will need unbreakable bonds of friendship to carry her through horrors and trials. And all of that still might not be enough.

Welcome to the moment that will save us or doom us all. Welcome to the War for Hope.
