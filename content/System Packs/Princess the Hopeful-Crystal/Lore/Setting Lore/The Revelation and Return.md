On July 20th, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first human beings to walk on the Moon. They didn’t do it for NASA, they didn’t do it for the United States of America, they didn’t do it for personal glory. They came
in peace for all mankind.

When Armstrong’s boot first imprinted the lunar dust, the Trap cracked. Again, it’s hard to determine why, as few knew what to be looking for at the time. Some scholars say this was the ultimate act of peace and aspiration. Some say it was an accomplishment that even [[The Fearless Kingdoms]] hadn’t achieved. Some think that it stirred up some lurking intangible creature and caused it to leave its guard post. But every human trapped in the Dreamlands heard him say those words: “That’s one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.” They knew they were imprisoned in a reflection of the real world. And they knew they needed out.

The Queen of Currents, of the glittering Danaan Archipelago. The Queen of Flowers, of the Tranquil Forest of Wen-Mung. The Queen of Jewels, of the Beautiful Palaces of Andarta. The Queen of Spades, of the Confederacy of Four Winds. And the Queen of Swords, of the Jungle Heart of Aztallan. They rose, they rallied, they beat back their Wardens and destroyed the Trap as soon as they knew of its existence. They explored outward into the Dreamlands and found lore lost to them, secrets long neglected. They were free of the Wardens, and still imprisoned. 

They were made of dreams. Their armies and kingdoms didn’t actually exist, not in the sense that they could come charging out of a portal and liberate the world. Worse yet, even though they knew they were living in Dreamlands, it’s the nature of dreams to sweep you up in their stories despite what you know of the real world. It’s a phenomenon called the Gales, and it meant that the Queens and their Kingdoms couldn’t orchestrate a plan of attack to rise and beat back the Darkness. They were and are Gale-Blind. For all their wisdom and grace, they can’t discern what they know about the real world from what they “know” with the same dream-certainty that you “know” a bulldog with your third grade teacher’s face will eat your soul if it catches you. To their great dismay, they couldn’t lead us directly into the Light no matter how powerful their yearning.

With the Trap shattered, the imprisoned souls in the Dreamlands could now individually escape and be reincarnated into reality. That would have to be their plan. Souls who were born here, not blinded by the Gales, who understood this world as natives. The Queens would have to nurture and guide a new generation of Noble as best
they could and entrust them with the task and their legacy. Some would Blossom on their own in moments of great joy or great need, empowered by just that little bit of extra light leaking into the world. Others would be sought out by shikigami, weak familiar spirits from the Dreamlands with the power to find and awaken Princesses.

The Twilight Queens also face this Renaissance. They have more potential recruits and converts than ever before. Each of them also believes they are the natural allies and leaders of the Radiant Queens and their Princesses, and
that they need only exert a little influence to get the Radiant to see it their way. And spontaneous Blossomings happen like never before among people in their first incarnations, illuminated by the brighter Light coming to the world.
