From there, the history of this world began, the [[Chronicles of Darkness]]. If you want the official story you can look at a history book. If you want the true story there’s at least eight types of supernatural creature claiming to offer it and none of them can see all of it. The time the Light was suppressed in the world is known as the Long Night. And even the powerful and knowledgeable huddled together in the darkness, in secret, fearful of the massive world outside their little communities and fearful of all the things they couldn’t know.

The Light of Hope is not something humanity has that can be taken away by an external power, it’s something that humanity is. But it can be suppressed, snuffed, choked away by the oppressive taint of fear. Those who hold it can be crushed, but there will always be a few who awaken its power, even with nobody around to hold as an example and nobody to help. Blossoming happened during the Long Night, but it’s pointless and reductive to say “oh, this or that figure from history was a Prince or Princess,” since the Light is what we all are. You can’t say that people did or didn’t need the Light to be good people because the Light is already in all of us.

And not all of the Noble were trapped in [[the Dreamland]]. Some escaped or endured in other ways, costing them their virtue and hope. There are at least four, though one of their number—a Twilight King—fell so long ago that little is known of him or his followers. The others are the three Twilight Queens. During the Long Night, the period
of the Radiant’s imprisonment in the Dreamlands, they were the only organized Noble presence in the world.

[[The Queen of Tears]] rules [[Campaign- City of Sin/Locations/The Dreamlands & Dark World/Twilight Kingdoms/Alhambra|Alhambra]], final bastion of [[The Fearless Kingdoms]], a city of eternal rainfall and gloom that yet stands as the last visible light in [[the Dark World]]. Fear has little purchase here, but neither does any other emotion. Alhambra is a city of depression, of weak and lifeless feeling, its existence only maintained by parasitism. The eternally dim lanterns of Alhambra burn hope in order to keep the city safe, and its Princes and Princesses venture into the real world to steal that hope away from humanity in order to keep the city lit another day. Alhambra is the last remaining testament to the beauty and goodness of the Fearless Kingdoms, and they see the struggle to fight the Darkness back as futile. The only thing they aspire to is to hold the one last thing that matters in their hands against the darkness, at any cost. They don’t enjoy what they must do, but they see the world, and they see the beauty of Alhambra even in its rain and sorrow, and they
know they must do it.

[[The Queen of Storms]] once ruled the powerful [[The Kingdom of Gonel|kingdom of Gonel]] as a wise and impartial judge, her love for her charges great, and her wrath at injustice as sudden and devastating as a bolt of lightning. When her followers felt cowardice and retreated from their duties, she did the only thing she could, which was to search out evil and destroy it. Over and over and over again. One foul thing slain led to the next, and the next. She gathered followers, but leading them wasn’t as important as slaying the enemy. They followed her even as her eyes grew tired and sunken, her once mighty roars hoarser and raspier, her thoughts ever more sparse and incogent. Her vitriolic hatred wore down her affinity for lightning along with her very soul. Her hands no longer called forth the light of the heavens, but instead the blistering acid that etched everything else about her away. She never left the Dark World or its limitless supply of lifeless monsters stirred up by her presence. Waging her personal war is all she does now. She doesn’t sleep, she doesn’t eat, she barely speaks. She is a one-woman crusade who happens to be followed by preaching acolytes.

[[The Queen of Mirrors]] did not fall into the [[The Trap and the Dream|Trap of the Dreamlands]]—she made her own. All that remains of her [[The Kingdom of Heril|kingdom of Heril]] is the uppermost level of her palace’s spire, unmoored in nothingness. She survives only in the reflection of her own radiant Light. She pines for the prophesied True Heir, the Noble among Nobles with
the power to win back the world from the Darkness with effortless grace. Her only purpose is to find this True Heir and foster them into the glorious hero who will make all the tragedy like it never happened. They say she looks out of every mirror in our world, endlessly searching for the potential Heir. Each Princess she takes under her wing, each one she mentors, she treats as if they are the only and obvious Heir, no matter how many others she is likewise supporting at the same time. She teaches them to worship themselves and to encourage others to worship them as well. She teaches that only their power and happiness matter, that she should be exalted and nobody can be her equal, because nobody else could possibly take on the unbearable weight of saving the world. Each one inevitably falls, and so she lies to herself that she never believed in them in the first place. She’s told the lie so often she believes it.

Each of them has fallen from their ideals and virtue; each has been worn down by compromises or blindness or denial. But during the Long Night, they were the only ones to follow. And each of them sincerely did what they thought was best.

When humans Blossomed, when one slipped through the cracks of the Trap and was reincarnated here, the Twilight Queens were all they had. The Queen of Tears was the only stability, the Queen of Storms the only driving purpose, and the Queen of Mirrors the only source of pride. They didn’t just offer someone to follow, some way to make sense of the world; they offered a way out of the horrible loneliness. They were the only choice to make.

Until the [[The Queens|the Radiant]] returned.
