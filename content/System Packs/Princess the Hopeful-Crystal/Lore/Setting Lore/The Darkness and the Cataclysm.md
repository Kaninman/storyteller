The [[Darkness]] existed back then; there is no light without darkness and there is no good without evil. Maybe Darkness and Light existed as counterparts when the universe was born. Light was instantiated as the Light of Hope in humankind, but Darkness just stayed itself. Whether or not the Darkness was the source of misery and hatred or its embodiment doesn’t really matter, for it loved all forms of suffering and sought to spread them.

But the Darkness was weak, easily defeated, for evil cannot create and can only destroy. What the hell was it going to make as its exemplars? How could it seduce the creatures of light when it had nothing to offer them? Evil isn’t powerful. Evil is pathetic and useless and futile, and in [[The Fearless Kingdoms]] that was clearly illuminated for everyone to see.

The Darkness has a will, but it doesn’t have thoughts. It simply is, without aspiration beyond the spiteful impulse to tear down and destroy. What little strength it gathered it would futilely throw at the Light, accomplishing nothing. So what happened wasn’t the Darkness’s own doing. It couldn’t have been, because it required a plan, an aspiration, the desire to change things – for the worse, not better. The creation of fear was the greatest betrayal possible, humans turning against humanity for no reward other than to make them suffer, and we don’t even know who it was.

We are pretty sure there were three figures who orchestrated the creation of fear, known them only by descriptive titles: the Red Queen of Contempt, the White Queen of Shame, and the Black Queen of Despair. We don’t know what they call themselves, or even if they ever were human enough to have identities. We think we know something each of them did to destroy the Fearless Kingdoms, but were there other “Dark Queens” we didn’t catch? Were they even different beings or just one thing with many masks? Even the existing records from back then, preserved in [[GOD MACHINE/System Packs/Princess the Hopeful-Crystal/Lore/Location Lore/Alhambra]]’s library, are spotty and incomplete. Dispassionate observation is nearly impossible in the moment when you, everyone you know, and everyone you don’t, are first introduced to fear.

The fall of the Fearless Kingdoms was not a slow decline into decadence and lost ideals. It was a Cataclysm; sudden, destructive, and omnipresent. With no warning, fear erupted like earthquakes along fault lines. No one understood what it was or why. Panic spread like wildfire in people who didn’t know what panic was, or how to deal with it. Just as love and kindness can surge outward and cause someone to Blossom, so too can fear create a Nightscape, a horrible realm of terror and torture crafted around its creator to both exalt and imprison them, birthing and loosing yet more shrieking, quivering creatures of terror, a spreading flood leaving more Nightscapes in their rippling wake. For all the many virtues of the Fearless Kingdoms, they had no courage. For the meaning of courage is not the absence of fear, it’s the ability to act despite it. How could they have known what that was, much less cultivate it?

Bonds of trust shattered and ideals suffocated under the assault of fear. Friends became enemies not because of what they had done but what they might do. The Red Queen of Contempt did not come riding with an invading force to destroy the Kingdoms. She rose among the people
and rallied them against each other, screaming “Destroy them! Slay them! They are cruel and greedy and want you to suffer! They aren’t as important as you! They don’t deserve love!” And the next day, she said the same thing to their neighbors, spreading hopeless violence outward.

The Noble fought against it. Immediately, vehemently, they did their utmost against the sudden and catastrophic existence of fear. They struggled through emotions they thought impossible, desperately trying to regain their
footing and establish a strategy. But they stumbled in their first efforts, not knowing how to approach the problem. And for each of them, when exhausted from effort, the White Queen of Shame appeared. Standing beside them to say “You can’t do it. You’re a sham, a fraud, a failure. You will never be able to save anyone, and everyone who ever depended on you should have known better than to believe in you. All you will do is let them down.”

The people looked to their leaders, to each other, trying to find answers and solidarity as their communities crumbled around them and the lights of their friends and loved ones were obscured by inky Darkness. Just as the pleas
they made went unanswered, the pleas they heard back were fearfully dismissed as the Darkness trying to trick them into vulnerability. The only voice they seemed to give credence to was the Black Queen of Despair, whispering only two words into each ear: “Give up.” 

We fell to blind fear, all of us, in one way or another. The Noble faced an assault by evil they were utterly unprepared for. This was not a timeless and mythic war across aeons. The world fell within 18 months. The Darkness won. It consumed nearly everything we had made and reduced it to a useless, desiccated husk. The only option was escape, hopefully to regroup and defeat it.
