# Queen

In addition to answering their Calling, Princesses also follow a Queen who exemplifies how she tends to approach her problems and her life. They tend to gravitate towards the Queen who best embodies their worldview and attitude, though sometimes this representation is not what
an outside observer would have expected. The followers of the Queens do fit general archetypes most of the time, but people will always surprise you.

A Queen is a leader, an ideal, an exemplar, and a community of followers to be in. Not simply an abstract idea or philosophy, she teaches and embodies her philosophy in deed and in personality. To have an audience with her
Queen, a Princess visits the Dreamlands. The path will always be easy to find see, meet, and talk to your Queen. Your Queen is like a parent, or a teacher, or a friend, who wants to see you succeed.

The Radiant Queens all suffer from a condition called [[Gale-Blind|Gale-Blindness]]. Just like you can realize you are dreaming but still be swept away, they know their existence in the Dreamlands isn’t “real” but it’s nearly impossible for them to differentiate whether what they remember is from the Dreamlands or from reality. Your Queen cannot help you by telling you exactly what to do or who to go to to solve a problem because she can only act on the information you personally bring to her. As mentors they guide you to find the solution by walking your own path rather than provide it for you.

## Why No Kings?

> All these leaders are presented as women, in line with the mahou shoujo genre, where generally these sorts of authority figures are women. They have distinct personalities and identities, so they can’t be presented as abstract principles that could be any gender. Of course, in your Chronicle you are free to determine some are men or nonbinary or whatever you decide, just as you can re-emphasize or change those personality traits (You are, however, forbidden from even implying Miguel is not a good boy). But it does raise the question: there was no gender discrimination in the Fearless Kingdoms, and nothing about your gender made you more or less virtuous or capable, so why are there eight Queens and no Kings? The odds of that seem low.
> There was a group called the Four Heavenly Kings. The Radiants were a tightly knit nakama even before the Cataclysm, and one more betrayed them in order to survive as a Twilight King. Their closeness and familiarity allowed these Kings to awaken and escape the Trap quickly, and they gathered their own followers and heroes in an era long, long since past. They confronted their Twilight counterpart and defeated him so badly that he had no choice but to become their friend, and then they all set out a quest of vital importance—and were never seen again. There could still be lost lore out there that illuminates their lives, and Nobles could seek out or Blossom into their ideals.
