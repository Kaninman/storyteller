The effect of the Gales has been reinforced on your spirit. Instead of distracting you from recalling memories of the real world, those memories are being displaced. For each stack of Gale-Lock, some of your memories have been altered and replaced with memories of a life in the Dreamlands. The more stacks, the more important the memories. At five stacks, your body is comatose and you remember nothing of your life in the real world. 

As you become more swept up in the dream narrative, focusing on specifics becomes harder. For each stack of Gale-Lock, you get -1 to all rolls you make that could be in line with embodying your Drive, but aren’t. You can’t have more stacks of Gale-Lock than [[Galemark]]. 

You can’t lose [[Galemark]] stacks if they’d take you below your Gale-Lock stacks.

**Cause:** You had existing [[Galemark|Galemarks]] that were locked by someone with the Dream Warden Numen. 

**Beat:** Take an opportunity to embody your Drive that you obviously wouldn’t have if you were thinking clearly. 

**Ending:** When the Warden responsible dies, all stacks of Gale-Lock are removed and you become completely and permanently immune to all of that Warden’s powers once it reforms. Your friends and allies can attempt to remove Gale-Lock stacks by fighting against the efforts of the Warden, presenting evidence of strong and important memories that are foundational to your identity, and succeeding on social rolls to convince you of their relevance.
