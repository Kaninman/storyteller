External or internal stress has sent you into a fuguelike state where your mind rejects messy reality, substituting the world in your head instead. You’re disconnected from reality, maybe even disconnected from your own body; some people even feel like they’re watching themselves from the outside.

You can’t keep track of specific information about a situation, and react to everything as if it was a stereotypical instance of some archetype you’re familiar with; you can attempt a Composure roll to overcome this for one specific action. Without that, you might do things like attacking or antagonizing others who LOOK threatening even if you should know they’re not, ignore what should be a higher priority to do something simple and easy to understand, utterly fail to understand nuance, perform a familiar action similar to what you were actually trying to do but not actually the same (like when you drive a car and notice yourself going to a familiar destination even though you’re actually going somewhere else), or otherwise go on “autopilot.”

**Causes:** Dissociation is “dormant” until a stressor appears in the current scene; it can be inflicted by Belief Compromises, the Warped Reflection Merit, or mundane sources of stress and trauma.

**Ending:** You’ll snap out of Dissociated when the stressor is no longer present, the scene ends, or someone deals damage to you for the explicit purpose of slapping you back to your senses.
