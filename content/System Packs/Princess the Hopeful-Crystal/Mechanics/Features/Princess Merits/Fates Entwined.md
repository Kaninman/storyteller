Destiny does not merely have a role for you—it has a role for two. Your destiny is entwined with a Storyteller character, and the universe will keep conspiring to put you two together and give you the chance to carry out a destined pairing. You might be destined lovers, you might be destined rivals, you might go from one to the other, but you’re going to run into each other again and again. Destiny can’t make you feel love, but it can give you the chance.

Choose a Storyteller character, or give an outline for the Storyteller to make one for you, to entwine your fate with. Taking the Fates Entwined Merit gives the same Merit to this Storyteller character. You can use your Destiny Points on any interaction with that character, no matter what the goal is—but your actions will never end up killing or permanently removing this character from your life without a huge destiny-charged event. The same applies to them; they can use Destiny on rolls with you (they have the same number of dots as you even if they don’t qualify for the Merit), and cannot kill or permanently remove you.

The Storyteller character you are entwined with will appear frequently in your life. If your entwined character has a double life, expect to run into them in both “lives.” No matter how obvious it is to you, the player, your character will remain completely oblivious to the link between the two unless bludgeoned over the head with it.
