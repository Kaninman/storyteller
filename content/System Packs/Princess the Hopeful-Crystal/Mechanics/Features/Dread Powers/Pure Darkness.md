This Horror is expressed through Pure Darkness, and not another magical idiom. This means that in terms of raw physical threat, it is absolutely coked-out compared to other monsters of similar Potency, because it hasn’t been “watered down” through intermediary magics or philosophies.

A creature of Pure Darkness has several bonuses based on its Potency:
★ Attacks against it suffer a penalty equal to its Potency. This is lowered by things that lower Defense, but isn’t bypassed by things that ignore Defense.
★ Its attacks ignore dice penalties from Defense (and other effects that make attacks miss) equal to its Potency. This can’t end up providing a bonus, just lower penalties (down to 0).
★ It has General Armor equal to half its Potency, rounded down.
★ It has Ballistic Armor equal to half its Potency, rounded up.
★ Its attacks pierce Armor by an amount equal to half its Potency, rounded down.
★ It gets a bonus to Initiative equal to its Potency.
★ It gets a bonus to non-combat uses of Dread Powers equal to its Potency.
★ If it uses the Simplified Horror rules, then instead of a specific bonus to Dread Powers and negating Defense, it gets a bonus to its Best At ability equal to its Potency. It gets a bonus equal to half its Potency, rounded up, to its All Other dice pools.
Any Horror with Pure Darkness has a Bane, even if it’s a type of creature that normally doesn’t have one. The Horror’s Bane is able to negate all these bonuses, and the specific form of the Bane will determine if the mere presence of the Bane is enough or if it must be struck with the Bane as an attack. Attacks to a monster’s weak point bypass the Armor and, depending on the monster, might turn off other abilities. The difficulty to find the Bane of a creature depends on its (base, unbuffed) Rank or Potency; if you adjust a creature’s stat block, make the Bane appropriately rarer or more common.

All creatures of Pure Darkness (Darkspawn, Nightmares, and Cataphracts) have this Dread Power for free. Other forms of Horror might also have it.

For Nightmares specifically, the bonus from Pure Darkness isn’t based on its own Potency, but rather the Potency of the Nightscape it’s from. Increasing or lowering the Nightscape’s Potency will alter the stats of its Nightmares.
