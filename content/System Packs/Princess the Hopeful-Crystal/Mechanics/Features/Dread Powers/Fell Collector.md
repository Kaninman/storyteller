The Horror collects its victims quite literally—converting them into dolls, certificates, snow globes, poker chips, or something else they can amass and play with at their leisure.

Like Soul Collector, this power requires the Horror to meet a condition to collect its victim; Unlike Soul Collector, this condition can be something that happens quickly, like “the victim must accept they have lost at a game against me” or “the victim must reveal their telltale mark of fear.” By spending three Willpower and overcoming the victim’s Resolve + Potency, the Horror imprisons them, converting them into a helpless object of Size 0-1 over the course of one turn, and applying the Objectified Condition (p. 270).

Depending on the form of the collectible, the victim may be able to act and move after being converted—a doll might sneak out and hide in the vents, even a snow globe can be rolled like a hamster ball—but don’t expect the Horror to just leave you around to do whatever you like. If the object is completely unable to act, then destroying it will usually free the person inside. The Fell Collector will have some other condition that frees people from the collectibles. This Dread Power also ends when the Horror is killed or beaten unconscious.
