When sapping willpower isn’t enough, some Horrors drain their victims’ vitality in a more direct way. Depending on the Horror, victims may feel slow and dull, may age or get younger, may become translucent or thicker—they change to reflect that some attribute is stolen from them, and there’s only one way to get it back.

A Horror with this Dread Power has one or two Attributes it can drain. The Horror may spend 1 Willpower while touching a victim to contest against the victim’s Stamina + Potency. On a success, it drains one Attribute dot from the victim and stores it. It may spend one Willpower to add all of its stored Attribute dots to its own Attributes in any combination or configuration it wishes—if it drains Composure, it can assign those dots to Strength or Dexterity if it wishes.

Stealing Attribute dots can also steal something else from the victim, like their voice, vision, or appearance. In this case, only one dot can be stolen from each victim. The Horror can hold a number of stolen Attribute dots equal to its Health. When it takes damage of any kind, each box filled frees up one stolen Attribute dot, which returns to its owner.
