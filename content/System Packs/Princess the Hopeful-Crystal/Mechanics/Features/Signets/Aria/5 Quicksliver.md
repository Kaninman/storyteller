You become so fast and slippery that even time can’t grasp you. Once per scene, you may reflexively spend one Willpower and one Wisp to freeze time. You take one turn in the frozen time during which nothing but you can move, interrupting another character’s action if necessary. You don’t encounter air resistance, but liquids act as solid surfaces unless you have another ability that would change that. Acting inside frozen time is difficult: when you make a roll to affect the outside world in this free turn, you have to spend one Wisp per each success that you want to keep. Rolled successes that you do not pay a Wisp for are lost.

If anyone else in the area has the ability to stop time, they may reflexively activate it when you activate this ability (and vice versa). If you’re matching an opponent’s time stop, you can do it even if you’ve already used Quicksilver in this scene. If this happens, Quicksilver lasts the entire duration of whatever time stop ability the other being uses, and you both move inside the frozen time, taking combat turns where only you two act. You don’t have to pay Wisps for successes on rolls made against others sharing this frozen time.
