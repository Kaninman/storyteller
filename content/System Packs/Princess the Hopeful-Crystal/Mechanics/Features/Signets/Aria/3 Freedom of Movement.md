When something prevents you from moving out of a physical location, you may reflexively spend a Wisp to ignore that effect. You can’t phase through solid objects, but you can escape any bindings, cross any hindering terrain, end any stasis or paralysis or compulsion to stay still, even momentarily escape the notice of a guard or monitor (you do not become undetectable, you just stop the moment they’d prevent you from leaving). Against magical effects, this provokes a Clash of Wills, but your effect gets the +4 bonus for long-lasting magic even though it only activated this turn. If it sucks—hit da bricks!
