Gifted to Princesses of Jewels—allows Empathy to be used instead of Medicine

This is that whole “mending broken hearts” thing, allowing you to treat a patient’s mental and psychological injury.

The injury you treat may now be “a Mental Condition or Tilt, or a memory being too vivid or not vivid enough.” The required successes are dependent on the Condition in question, with more complex and long-lasting Conditions taking more successes and Tilts only taking one or two. The interval between rolls extends to one day, each roll costs a Wisp, and you can interrupt and resume your cast as many times as you want. (Note that this only applies to casts that treat Conditions, and not memories.)

If you finish treating a Persistent Condition, and it wasn’t applied in the current scene, the patient has to sacrifice a Willpower dot to avoid it coming back after one day per Inner Light of the caster. This can only remove ego-dystonic Conditions (those against the patient’s personality and self-image).

Memories can’t be altered, but they can be made more or less important. The successes for the Charm allow you to place an equally sized bonus or penalty to the Intelligence + Composure roll to recall certain memories; penalties represent a memory becoming less important and bonuses represent it becoming more important. Without either Rewinding or Caustic Restoration, you can’t erase memories altogether. The patient can always spend a Willpower point to counter a cast of Soothing Restoration (which will not prevent you from casting again but does use up any resources you had), so ask first.
