Gifted to Princesses of Tears—allows Stealth to be used instead of Subterfuge

An image can’t appear to be nothing, but that doesn’t mean it can’t be used to hide. If they can’t see through the illusion, they might suspect there’s something on the other side but not know what. Obscuring Illusion creates an image that fills an area with something that blocks perception: a cloud of shadow, a freezing mist, a blinding light, and the like. When you cast Obscuring Illusion, choose if it inflicts the Poor Light Tilt (p. 273) or the full Blinded Tilt (CofD p. 281) on everyone who hasn’t seen through the image. Physical interaction cannot dispel it because it does not appear to have physical mass without Blunt Illusion, but Blunt Illusion can make it feel like a sandstorm or something along those lines.

An Obscuring Illusion can be extended to 10 times the normal area of your image.
