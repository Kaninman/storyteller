A Grace needs to be able to touch the hearts of others to move them, and to do that, she usually makes a heartfelt Plea about her dreams, her values, her hopes. By showing who she truly is inside, she can sway others to her cause. This Charm is not capable of compelling or forcing any behavior; rather, a Plea offers another person information and incentives, and assists with mundane attempts at persuasion – but ultimately the decision must be theirs. Princesses are incapable of mind control and the Light of Hope is incapable of disrespecting another person so deeply.

# Plea Mechanics

**Action:** Extended. Each roll is one turn.
**Dice Pool:** Presence + Persuasion + Inner Light—target’s Composure.
**Duration:** One scene. Scaling: The successes rolled on the Charm are the effectiveness of your persuasion or Social Maneuvering test.
**Exceptional Success:** Like with Social Maneuvering, an Exceptional Success allows you to open two Doors.

**Basic Action:** You beseech an audience, asking them to perform a request and/or a favor. You initiate a special Social Maneuver, rolling to beseech them each turn. On the first successful roll, the request is granted, and the scale of the effect is based on the successes rolled. When all of the audience’s Doors are opened and the Social Maneuver is successful, the favor is granted.

## Plea Info

★ To **beseech** is to “communicate an argument or persuasive attempt by talking to someone.”
★ Your **audience** is “one individual who can hear and understand you.”
★ A **request** is “Please perform this action that you can and would be willing to do as the result of a simple persuasion roll.”
★ A **favor** is “Please perform this action that you can and would be willing to make as the result of an extended Social Maneuvering attempt.”

The Social Maneuvering roll caused by Plea is different from other Social Maneuvering rolls. The Impression of the audience does not determine the interval at which you can make maneuvering rolls; instead, like in Psyche combat (p. 162), it determines the required successes to open a Door:

| Impression | Example                                                          | Target |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| Perfect    | Someone whose life you've saved                                  | 1      |
| Excellent  | Someone personally impressed by you and your deeds.              | 2      |
| Good       | Someone who has heard of and believes in you and your mission.   | 3      |
| Average    | Someone who thinks you look awfully silly but probably harmless. | 4      |
| Hostile    | Someone actively opposed to your efforts                         | 5\*    |

> Ordinarily plea fails on hostile targets, the target number is there for upgrades that bypass this.

Plea may also be used to augment Psyche combat. If used in Psyche combat, instead of altering how Impression functions, this Charm allows excess successes over the ones required to open a Door to count towards opening the next Door, and can add a **request** and **favor** to the attack. The request still has to make sense as part of a negotiation, and the favor is granted once the target’s Psyche damage track is filled with damage, if still applicable— the Nightscape’s prisoner will be able to hear you out, but a Nightmare will have mere moments before it dissolves into frightful nothingness.

You cannot force open Doors while using Plea. Upgrades allow you to add multiple components to the request and favor; each addition to the request after the first lowers your dice pool by -1 and each addition to the favor after the first increases the audience’s Doors by 1.

★ Invocation: The Plea Charm requires communication with another entity. The act of communicating is sufficient for Invoking the Charm, so long as you clearly care about what you are saying.
★ Regalia: Plea Regalia can be things that directly help you transmit your message (those little stage microphones you stick to your cheek are always an option), but more often takes the form of something that helps get your message across or indicates you should be taken seriously and listened to. Cosmetics like rouge, lipstick, blush, anything that makes you look good can be Regalia to make people pay attention to your message; so can formal wear, a corsage, a tie, even a lab coat that helps sell your intelligence. (There are rumors of some extremely detail-oriented Princesses of Currents or Jewels with charts, whiteboards, or magical PowerPoint presentations. Take of those what you will.)
