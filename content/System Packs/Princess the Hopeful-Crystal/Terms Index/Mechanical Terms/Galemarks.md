A character is subject to [[The Gales]] once per scene in the Dreamlands, or when another effect specifies (notably the [[Gale-Wracker]] Numina, p. 230). When a character is subject to the Gales, they roll Belief. If the number of successes they get is more than their current Galemark stacks, they gain a stack of [[Galemark]] (p. 270). If it isn’t, then they add +1 to subsequent rolls until they get another stack.

Like [[Shadows]] from [[Sensitivity#Chronic Sensitivity|Chronic Sensitivity]], the first Galemark is basically free—it gives you only a slight penalty and it’s well within your ability to tolerate. You’re a little disoriented: you have -1 to recall specific information from the real world, and get -1 to recall specific information from here when you return to the real world, but you’re fine. Mostly.

Every stack of Galemark after the first gives you another -1 penalty to recalling information between worlds, and also a -1 penalty to every single action you take if it’s not in line with fully embodying (i.e. regaining all Willpower for) your Drive. This is only for things you proactively do, not passive things like perception, or defensive rolls.

Upon waking, you lose one stack of Galemark. If you still have 2 or more, dream-logic continues to infringe on your thoughts and mess with you, and you keep losing track of details of reality. Each subsequent day you have a full night’s sleep, you don’t dream at all, don’t recover a Willpower, and clear one more stack of Galemark. Galemarks are also Mental Conditions, so they can be healed by [[Soothing Restoration]], but you can still only heal them in the real world.
