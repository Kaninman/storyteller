The Gales are the force of [[the Dreamlands]]’ logic to draw you in and make you subject to the Dreamlands’s rules. Like the wind, while the Gales blow there’s nothing you can do to stop them, only react to them. Like the wind, they shape the landscape over time. And like the wind, they can change rapidly.

The Gales are both the power and the fluidity of the narrative of our dreams, the stories we tell ourselves to understand and create our aspirations. The Gales are why dream-logic makes sense. The Gales blow things around
outside your perception to keep everything changing. The Gales whisper in your ear, but what they whisper isn’t “give up” or “throw away your life for fantasy.” It’s “this all makes perfect sense,” “don’t notice those details,” and “you vividly remember the President being a dog.”

The risk of being caught up in the Gales is not being caught up in someone else’s story; it’s being too caught up in your own story to notice you’ve left those of others. It’s listening to the story you tell yourself, about yourself, to the exclusion of reality. Your Drive is the thing you’re About, and the Gales can’t blow that down—but they blow down every- thing else that ties you to the real world and makes you a fully realized person
